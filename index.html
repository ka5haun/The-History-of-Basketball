The History of Basketball

Basketball was invented in December 1891 by James Naismith(image above) in Springfield, Massachusetts. It started off as a game where soccer balls were thrown into peach baskets. Shortly after, the first official game of basketball was played on March 11, 1892. At that time, there was nine players on each team; there is now five on each team which is one way that shows how much the game has changed since then. In 1896, the first college game was played, which marked a milestone in the progression of the popularity. The NBL was made in 1898, but ended briefly only lasting five years. In the 1930s, basketball became an Olympic sport. This was a big deal because it was now a global game that many countries took seriously. In 1949, the NBA was created which still exists today. Chuck Cooper was the first African American to be drafted into the NBA in 1950 which marked the end of segregation in the league.

The Original 13 Rules of Basketball

  1. The ball may be thrown in any direction with one or both hands.
  2. The ball may be batted in any direction with one or both hands (never with the fist).
  3. A player cannot run with the ball.
  4. The ball must be held in or between the hands; the arms or body must not be used for holding it.
  5. No shouldering, holding, pushing, striking, or tripping an opponent.
  6. A foul is striking the ball with the fist.
  7. If either side makes three consecutive fouls, it counts as a goal for the opponent.
  8. A goal is made when the ball is thrown or batted from the ground into the basket and stays there.
  9. When the ball goes out of bounds, it shall be thrown back into the field of play by the person who touched it last.
  10. The time shall be two 15-minute halves, with a five-minute rest between.
  11. The team scoring the most goals in that time shall be declared the winner.
  12. In case of a tie, there shall be an additional five-minute period of play.
  13. The rules may be altered at any time by the referee.
